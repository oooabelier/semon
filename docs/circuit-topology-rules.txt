semon circuit topology rules

1. Circuits contain direction-agnostic pins, buses, virtual 3-pin gates, and subcircuit instances.
2. A pin is not an input or output. Locked values drive propagation in whichever direction the local constraints imply.
3. Every generated basic circuit is normalized to Virtual3PinGate constraints.
4. A Virtual3PinGate has up to three pins and an 8-bit Truth-Byte. Bit index CBA selects the validity of a pin-state combination.
5. Truth-Byte 0xFF is universal freedom and is skipped by propagation. Truth-Byte 0x00 is universal contradiction and aborts through diagnostics.
6. Buffers and inverters are emulated with the equality and inverted equality truth tables, not separate component types.
7. Composite circuits may instantiate existing circuits structurally. Each instance has independent pin state, locks, and boundary routing.
